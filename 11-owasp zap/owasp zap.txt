Since owasp zap was not preinstalled on my kali live usb, I began by installing the package manually. Once the installation was complete, I launched the application and proceeded to run an automated scan against dvwa. In the automated scan tab, I entered the target url http://127.0.0.1/dvwa and initiated the attack. The scan took approximately one minute to complete, during which zap crawled the dvwa site and tested for common vulnerabilities. When the process finished, the results displayed a total of twelve alerts, five rated at medium severity, four at low severity and three categorized as informational risks. These findings provided a clear overview of dvwa’s exposed weaknesses and served as the foundation for subsequent manual testing and exploitation exercises.
